Anti-Semitic Crime on the Rise, NYPD Reports

Figures presented by NYPD report that hate crimes in New York City have risen in 2019.

A woman holds a sign up at a protest. Photo by T. Chick McClure on Unsplash.

As the end of 2019 approaches, the New York Police Department has reported that hate crimes have risen 33 percent in New York City. The rise is attributed to a 53 percent increase in anti-Semitic incidents, a 92 percent increase in anti-White incidents, eight percent increase in crimes motivated by sexual orientation and a seven percent increase in hate crimes against black people.

2019 Hate Crimes in NYC

In the rest of New York, the rise of anti-Semitic crime has become a pattern. The FBI suggested that hot-button political topics could be attributed to the increase of crimes against others. In 2018, hate crimes in America reached a 16-year high.

A recent shooting in nearby Jersey City has again brought anti-Semitism back into public discussion. Recently, President Trump signed an executive order in an attempt to combat anti-Israel protests on campus, a movement that critics call anti-Semitic.
